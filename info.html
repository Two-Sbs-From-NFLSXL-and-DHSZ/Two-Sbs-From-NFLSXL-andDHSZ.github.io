A-Level

     Advanced level is subject-based test offered by several organizations like AQA, OCR and Edexcel. As part of General Certificate of Education(GCE), A-level test is seen as a school-leaving qualification test and is required for university entrance in the United Kingdom. It takes approximately two years for students to accomplish A-level test, each year taking three to four subjects. Unlike other university-entrance tests, students are allowed to combine their favoirte subjects together since A-level has no subject requirements. Besides Britain, other countries, such as Singapore, Uganda and Kenya, have also developed their own A-level systems, which only have slight difference with that in the Britain.

Advanced Placement

     Advanced Placement is a program created and organized by College Board. The program offers high school students college-level courses and exams based on these courses. Students who gain high scores on AP exams would be provided with course credits and placements by US unversities and colleges. AP exams begin on the first Monday of May and last two weeks, and exams of about thirty subjects would be held during this time. AP exams are scored on a 1~5 scale, where 1 is the lowest, meaning the student is unfamiliar with the testing subject, while 5 is the highest, and the student has fully understood the content of the course. The scores of AP exams would be published in July, and students have opportunities to cancel their scores if they find them unsatisfying.

SAT Test

     SAT test is a standardized test held by College Board. As one of the two major tests for American college addmission, SAT mainly tests whether students have sufficient ability in applying for colleges. SAT test is divided into three parts: reading, writing and language, and mathematics, with optional essay. It is held in March, April, May, June, August, October and December every year. SAT takes roughly three hours to finish, and the extra essay section takes another 50 minutes to accomplish. The score ranges from 400 to 1600, and the students usually gets their test scores after three weeks after the test is held.

ACT Test

     ACT test is a standardized test for US university and college admission, held by ACT, a nonprofit organization. It tests students knowledge in four perspectives: English, maths, reading, and science reasoning, each part of which is counted independently, with a score between 1 and 36. ACT test is available for students in February, April, June, July, September, October and December every year. The total time of an ACT test is typically 3 hours, but it has much more questions compared to SAT, so students are required to have the ability to finish each question in a very limited time.
