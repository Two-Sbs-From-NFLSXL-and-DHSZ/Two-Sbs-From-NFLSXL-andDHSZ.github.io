Duration

SAT test begins at 8:30 to 9:00 am on the test(if the test is not postponed), the process of SAT test is like this:

  • Reading: 65 min
  • Break: 10 min
  • Writing and Language: 35 min
  • Math I (no calculator): 25 min
  • Break: 5 min
  • Math II (calculator required): 55 min
  • Break: 2 min
  • Essay (optional): 50 min

Structure of Test

  • Reading
    • Passages: 5 passages, including an extract from novel, a historical document, two social science papers, and a science paper
    • Number of Questions: 52 multiple choices
    • What to Test: students ability of understanding the passage
  • Writing and Language
    • Passages: 4 passages
    • Number of Questions: 44 multiple choices, each passage has 11 questions
    • What to Test: grammar and reading abilities
  • Math I: No Calculator
    • Number of Questions: 15 multiple choices and 5 fill-in blanks
    • What to Test: basic algebraic knowledge
  • Math II: Calculator Required
    • Number of Questions: 30 multiple choices and 8 fill-in blanks
    • What to Test: math knowledge including algebra, basic statistics, and geometry

Score

The 5 parts of SAT test is counted separately and added together:

  • Reading: 100~400
  • Writing and Language: 100~400
  • Math I: 100~400
  • Math II: 100~400
  • Essay: 2/2/2~8/8/8 (counted by three perspectives: reading/analysis/writing and the score is counted independently)

The total score of SAT test is 400~1600. The count of score in each part of the test follows a curve, which is totally different depending on the time and the place where the test is held.

Recommend Score

The recommend SAT score is different for each college. Some colleges and universities do not require students to send a SAT score to them, but some require students to have a SAT score. It is always recommended for high school students to have a high SAT score, and a score of more than 1400 could be very beneficial when applying for colleges. Some colleges also require or recommend students to have a SAT Essay score.

Test Time and Test Center

SAT tests are usually held in the first Saturday March, April, May, June, August, October and December. SAT test is available all over the world, including North America, Europe and Asia. For more information about SAT test time and test centers, please click here .

Requirements and Advice

On test day, you should bring:

  • Your admission ticket.
  • A calculator. Click here to find out more about allowed calculators.
  • No. 2 pencils and erasers. This is the only type of pen you could bring.

You are suggested to bring:

  • A watch so you could keep up with the time.
  • Snacks and water. Since you have to have a up-to-4-hour test, you need to eat and drink during breaks to make yourself energetic.
  • A phone, but it could only be kept by the supervisor during your test time, or it would be seen as plagiarism and your score might be cancelled.

You are not allowed to bring:

  • Audio players, cameras, laptops, and any other devices that could be used for recording or transmission.
  • Notebooks, rulers, highlighers and colored pens.
  • Dictionaries

If you bring anything above, you might have your score cancelled.
