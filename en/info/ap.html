Duration

Each subject of AP tests has a different duration. The duration of some popular subjects are listed below:

  • Calculus AB: 3h 15m
  • Calculus BC: 3h 15m
  • Microeconomics: 2h 10m
  • Macroeconomics: 2h 10m
  • Physics 1: Algebra-Based: 3h
  • United States History: 3h 15m
  • Statistics: 3h
To find out the duration about more subject, please click here .

Subjects of Test

AP exam contains more than 30 subjects, including subjects in literature, science, social science and humanity. Some popular AP subjects are United States History, Calculus, World History, Statistics and English Language and Composition. To find out more about subjects of AP test, visit AP Offical Site .

Score

The score of AP tests is divided into 5 grades: 5: Extremely well qualified 4: Very well qualified 3: Qualified 2: Possibly qualified 1: No recommendation

The score of students would be transformed into the table above based on the average scoring of students taking the subject and the number of students taking the exam. Different subjects might have a totally different scoring system.

Recommend Score

Usually, a AP score of 4 or 5 is useful when applying for American colleges as well as exchanging course credits as students enter the college. If a score of lower than 4 is received, it is highly unrecommended to report the score to the colleges that you want to apply for. To apply for some top colleges and universities, a student should have at least 3 to 4 usable AP scores.

Test Time and Test Center

AP exams begin from the first Monday of May annually, and last for 2 weeks. Your test center for AP tests are associated with your school. To find out specfic test time for each subject you have registered, please click here .

Requirements and Advice

On test day, you should bring:

  • A calculator if required. Check Calculator Policies here.
  • No. 2 pencils with erasers.
  • Pens with black or dark-blue ink.
  • Your student ID.

You are suggested to bring:

  • A watch to keep up with time.
  • A ruler for drafting.
