Duration

ACT test is divided into 5 parts:

  • English: 45 minutes
  • Math: 60 minutes
  • Break
  • Reading: 35 minutes
  • Science: 35 minutes
  • Break
  • Writing (optional): 40 minutes

Structure of Test

  • English: 75 multiple choices in 5 passages
  • Math: 60 multiple choices
  • Reading: 40 multiple choices in three standalone passage and one paired passage
  • Science: 40 multiple choices in 6 to 7 passages

Score

The 5 parts of SAT test is counted separated, each of the four parts range from 1~36, and the writing section ranges from 2~12. The total score of ACT test is calculated from the average score of the four sections. Click here to learn more about the calculation of ACT test.

Recommend Score

American colleges recommend their students to have a ACT test score of higher than 27, and some top colleges recommend applicants to have a 32-or-higher ACT score so that they could have more benefits when applying for the school.

Test Time and Test Center

ACT tests are usually held in February, April, June, July, September, October and December. ACT test is available all over the world, including North America, Europe and Asia. For more information about ACT test time and test centers, please click here .

Requirements and Advice

On test day, you should bring:

  • Your ticket.
  • Your photo identification.
  • No.2 pencil.
  • Calculator. Click here to see allowed calculator types.

You are suggested to bring:

  • A watch to keep up the time.
  • Snacks to eat during breaks.

You are not allowed to bring:

  • Textbooks and notes
  • Dictionaries
  • Scratch paper
  • Colored pens
  • Any electric devices
